Module 1: Understanding the Industry

Objectives:

• Learn the types of modeling and which fit you best.

• Understand how agencies, clients, and photographers operate.

• Debunk myths and set realistic expectations.

Topics:

1. Types of Modeling:

Runway/High Fashion: Tall, lean. Walks in shows, wears designer collections.

Editorial: Seen in magazines, unique looks often desired.

Commercial: Smiling, relatable people in ads, catalogues.

Lifestyle: Family scenes, fitness, travel, real-life situations.

Fitness/Sports: Muscular, healthy, energetic.

Parts Modeling: Hands, feet, hair, legs for product ads.

Plus Size / Curve: Fuller figure (US size 12–16).

Petite: Women under ~5’7” in specific niches.

Alternative/Creative: Tattoos, piercings, non-mainstream.

2. Industry Roles:

Model: Talent in front of the camera.

Agency: Represents models, books jobs.

Client: Brand/company hiring for campaign.

Photographer: Creates the images.

Stylist/MUA: Style and prepare the model.

3. Reality Check:

• Most models freelance or supplement income.

• Rejection is common — not personal.

• Professionalism is just as important as looks.

Assignment:

• Make a list of modeling categories you might fit into.

• Research 3–5 models with a similar look to you.
